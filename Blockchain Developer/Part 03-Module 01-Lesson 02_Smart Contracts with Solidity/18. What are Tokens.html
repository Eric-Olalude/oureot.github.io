18. What are Tokens?

# What are Tokens?

By the end of this lesson, you will create your own token that you can send to any Ethereum address! But first, you might be wondering, what is a token? Let's talk through the purpose of tokens and how they are used in the Ethereum ecosystem.

Ethereum DApps, Ether, and Tokens

One of the differentiating features of Ethereum is that it’s not just a currency, it’s a smart contract platform. This means that we can build projects and DAPPS (decentralized applications) on its blockchain via smart contracts.

While Ethereum does have its own currency, ether, an important concept to understand is that they are different from tokens and they serve a different purpose.

Ether - The native currency of Ethereum used to power smart contracts

Tokens - Built on the Ethereum platform can be used for exchanging value between different parties based on rules specified in that token's smart contract

CryptoKitties - Tokens as a Trade-able Good

Tokens in the Ethereum ecosystem can represent any fungible (replaceable) tradable good such as coins, loyalty points, gold certificates, IOUs or not fungible goods (unique collectibles) such as in-game items, etc. The widely popular blockchain game CryptoKitties is an ideal example of fungible tokens. Each cryptokitty collectible is a unique digital asset - a token. This token can be claimed by an owner, sold, and bought.

## Recap

  • Smart contracts are used to create Tokens. These smart contracts also facilitate transactions of tokens and record balances of tokens in an account.
  • After a token has been created, it can be traded, spent, or given to someone else.
